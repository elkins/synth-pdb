## Acknowledgments

synth-pdb builds upon the following excellent scientific software:

- **Biotite**: Protein structure manipulation ([Kunzmann & Hamacher, 2018](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12859-018-2367-z))
- **OpenMM**: Molecular dynamics engine ([Eastman et al., 2017](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pcbi.1005659))
- **NumPy**: Numerical computing ([Harris et al., 2020](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-020-2649-2))
- **Numba**: JIT compilation ([Lam et al., 2015](https://doi.org/10.1145/2833157.2833162))

## Related Publications

If you're interested in the scientific methods implemented in synth-pdb, see:

- **Lipari-Szabo Formalism**: [Lipari & Szabo (1982)](https://doi.org/10.1021/ja00381a009) - NMR relaxation theory
- **Ramachandran Plots**: [Ramachandran et al. (1963)](https://doi.org/10.1016/S0022-2836(63)80023-6) - Protein backbone geometry
- **Dunbrack Rotamer Library**: [Dunbrack & Cohen (1997)](https://doi.org/10.1002/(SICI)1097-0134(199703)27:3<329::AID-PROT1>3.0.CO;2-8) - Side-chain conformations
- **MolProbity Validation**: [Lovell et al. (2003)](https://doi.org/10.1002/prot.10286) - Structure quality assessment
